2.2 — magic triangle

The magic triangle is a technique used to create a creative space by limiting yourself with three rules. These can include:

  • colour
  • tools
  • techniques
  • motive
  • style
  • [...]

I was also sick for this one and tried it at home. I found this exercise to be a little more tricky. Generally, I really enjoy limitations in art because they allow for new ways of thinking and for breaking old patterns. For example, I cannot make the sky blue if I am only allowed to use pink and orange markers. I also really enjoy drawing prompts because they give you the freedom to get very creative within these limitations.

Thinking of these limitations myself, however, I found challenging, because then it feels like no limitations at all. If I set my own boundaries, it is easy to define them in a way that does not actually limit me and therefore does not challenge me.

I tried Katharina’s example:

  1. motive: face
  2. tool: black pen
  3. style: do not put down the pen

Then I set my own example:

  1. motive: happy frogs
  2. tool: a green marker and a green fineliner
  3. limitation: I had to close my eyes while drawing

I could not see that my marker ran out of ink.
