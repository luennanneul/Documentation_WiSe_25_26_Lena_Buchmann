2.1 — morphological box

The morphological box is a technique used to come up with fun and creative combinations of things or variations of the same thing.
You choose three parameters and three variables for each of the parameters. Then you combine them randomly.

Being sick when we initially tried this exercise did not stop me from trying it out on my own. I chose the parameters: upper body [human, snail, frog], lower body [octopus, shark, eel] and number of eyes [1,2,9]. Then I used dice to choose my fate.
I was a little disappointed that I only got to draw one eye once, and on one occasion time I switched around upper and lower body animal because I was getting a little bored of the same features. My favourite animals are probably the snail-headed creatures. I found this exercise to be mainly fun (for example for character creation) but I do wonder, in which other areas this method could be applied to generate creative ideas.
